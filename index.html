Experience

Depactor

Toronto, Canada

Co-Founder

Feb. 2020 - Sept. 2020

Depactor was a cybersecurity start-up I founded through the University of Toronto Hatchery’s NEST program. Our start-up developed software that was used to detect business email compromise (BEC) attacks, a form of cybercrime that uses impersonation and spoofing type email fraud to scam organizations.

We caught these attacks by applying linguistic analysis and machine learning to attribute authorship of corporate emails to a 95% accuracy. You can think of this attribution as cross checking a “linguistic fingerprint”. We built this fingerprint based on lexical (ex: average sentence length), syntactical (ex: word frequency), and structural (ex: indentation) features found in past samples of a person’s writing.

Depactor was admitted to the Hatchery NEST 2020 Cohort, a 4-month University of Toronto start-up incubator. Obtained $14 000 CAD in fellowship funding, temporary office space, access to a prototype fund, and an Advisory Board. We were recognized as one of the cohort’s top start-ups qualifying as NEST finalists and pitched at the Hatchery’s 2020 Demo Day.
